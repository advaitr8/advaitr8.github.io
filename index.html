I am a development economist and Bayesian statistician. Presently I work as a Research Associate at the Behavioral Development Lab (housed in J-PAL South Asia). Our project is at the intersection of behavioral economics and mental health research. We study how clinical depression affects people's economic lives, beliefs, and preferences. I earned an MS in Economics from the New School for Social Research and a BSc in Economics, Mathematics, and Statistics from St. Joseph's College, Bangalore University.
