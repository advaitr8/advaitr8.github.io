i Advait R. Aiyer

I study education markets in the developing world using tools from applied microeconomics and empirical industrial organization. My current research focuses on how pricing, regulation, and institutional incentives shape access and quality in centralized college admissions systems in India.
I'm in the fifth year of the PhD in Applied Economics program at The Wharton School of The University of Pennsylvania. I am also the 2024-25 Sobti Family Doctoral Fellow at Penn's Center for the Advanced Study of India.
Previously, I worked as a J-PAL Research Associate at the Behavioral Development Lab. I earned an MS in Economics from the New School for Social Research and a BSc. in Economics, Mathematics, and Statistics from St. Joseph's College, Bangalore University.
